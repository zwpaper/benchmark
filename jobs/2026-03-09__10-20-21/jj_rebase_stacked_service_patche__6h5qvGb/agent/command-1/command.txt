## Tips

- `jj rebase -r <rev> -d <dest>` rebases a **single** revision (not its descendants) onto the destination. This is different from `-s`/`--source`, which also rebases descendants.
- Since commits D and E are leaf commits (no descendants), `-r` and `-s` behave identically here, but `-r` is more precise for single-commit rebases.
- After rebasing, jj automatically moves any bookmarks that pointed to the original commit to the new rebased commit. You do not need to manually run `jj bookmark set`.
- The `-` suffix in revsets (e.g. `services/auth-patch-`) means "parent of"; use this to inspect the parent of a bookmarked commit.
- Revset `x::y` means "all commits reachable from x to y" (inclusive of both endpoints if they are connected).
